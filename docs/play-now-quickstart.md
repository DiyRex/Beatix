# PLAY NOW — Your First Mix (free, no console)

You have everything you need: Rekordbox (free), the keyboard mapping, and the
mouse for EQ. Rekordbox's free plan runs 2 decks on-screen with NO hardware.
This guide gets you from zero to your first blend.

## A. One-time setup (5 min)
1. Open Rekordbox. Top-left, switch to **Performance / EXPORT** mode (the view
   with two decks + waveforms). This works without a controller.
2. Preferences → Keyboard → import & select **KbdDJ 2-Deck** (on your Desktop).
   - (Optional) add FX: click the (+) on FX1 On/Off → press `4`; Increase
     Parameter 1 → `5`; Decrease Parameter 1 → `R`.
3. Drag 4–6 songs you KNOW into a playlist. Pick tracks with a steady beat.
   Let Rekordbox analyze them (it finds the BPM + beatgrid automatically).

## B. The keys you actually need first
```
Browse list:     Up / Down arrows
Load to Deck A:  Shift + Left        Load to Deck B:  Shift + Right
Play / Pause:    Z (Deck A)          / (Deck B)
Cue:             A (Deck A)          ' (Deck B)
Beat Sync:       Q (Deck A)          ] (Deck B)
EQ knobs:        drag with the MOUSE (HIGH / MID / LOW)
Crossfader:      drag with the MOUSE   (or volume faders C/X and , / .)
```

## C. Your first mix — follow this exactly
1. Load a track to **Deck A** (Shift+Left). Press **Z** to play it. Push the
   crossfader fully LEFT so you only hear Deck A.
2. Load a second track to **Deck B** (Shift+Right).
3. Press **]** (Beat Sync on B) — now B's tempo + beats LOCK to A. This is the
   magic that lets you skip manual beatmatching while learning.
4. On Deck B, with the mouse **cut the LOW EQ all the way down** (so its bass
   won't clash with A).
5. Watch A's waveform. When it reaches a **phrase change** (see section D),
   press **/** to start Deck B in time.
6. Slowly slide the **crossfader toward the center** so both play together.
7. On the next phrase boundary, **bring Deck B's LOW EQ back up** and **lower
   Deck A's LOW EQ** — you've swapped the bass cleanly. That's a real transition.
8. Finish sliding the crossfader fully RIGHT. Deck A is now free to load the
   next song. Repeat. You're DJing.

## D. Phrasing (the thing that makes it sound pro)
Dance music is built in blocks of **8 / 16 / 32 beats**. Mixes sound clean when
you make changes ON those boundaries, not randomly.
- Rekordbox shows beat markers + a phrase/beat counter on the waveform — use
  your EYES as your ears while learning. Count groups of 8.
- Rule of thumb: bring the new track in at the START of a phrase, and do your
  EQ/fader swap on the next boundary.

## E. About headphones
Pro DJs pre-listen to the next track in headphones. With one laptop output you
can't split that yet — that's fine. Use the **on-screen waveforms** to line
things up and SYNC to match tempo. You can mix great this way to learn.

## F. Daily 15-minute practice
1. Pick 4 tracks at similar BPM. 2. Load → Sync → low-cut → bring in on a phrase
→ swap bass → crossfade out. 3. Do it 5 times. That single move, repeated, is
80% of DJing. Speed and feel come automatically.

You do NOT need a console for any of this. Go play.
